ATTENTION:

Don"t let the caliper hang freely, as the weight of the caliper puts stress on the brake lines and could damage them. 

What if the tool doesn"t press the piston back into the caliper?

This could be due to the direction at which the piston spins, as usually, kits have left- and right-hand threaded shafts to accommodate both, try the other and see if that presses it back in.

If not, ensure the cable mechanism is properly disassembled. If it still won"t move back, there"s a very good chance that the piston is seized and the caliper requires either rebuilding or replacement.

Ensure the threaded bar is well lubricated with molybdenum disulphide grease before use.

Front caliper piston: the operation is to use the disk plane, parallel top into the return position;

Rear caliper piston: it is necessary to distinguish between left and right rotation, selecting the adapter corresponding to the piston and rotating to screw the piston into the return position.
